Jalal Safari Bazargani

I'm an XR researcher at Super Realistic XR Research Center in Seoul, where I work on interaction techniques in VR/AR as well as immersive applications in domains such as education and healthcare. I am doing my PhD at Sejong University, where I am advised by Soo-Mi Choi.

Current Work

Currently, I have several works under review, including three papers submitted to IEEE VR 2026, one to CVM 2026, and manuscripts at the Entertainment Computing and Virtual Reality journal. These works span diverse areas such as proactive virtual agents, advanced search techniques in VR, and other emerging topics in immersive technology.

Research

I develop immersive technologies at the intersection of VR, AR, and AI, with applications ranging from education to healthcare. My goal is to translate research into impactful real-world innovations. Some papers are highlighted.
Virtual Reality-Assisted Visualization of Flood Susceptibility Using Optimized Machine Learning Models
Seyyed Vahid Razavi-Termeh, Jalal Safari Bazargani, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Houbing Song, Soo-Mi Choi
Applied Water Science, 2025
Status: Recently Accepted

A novel approach that exploits the immersive experience of Virtual Reality (VR) that engages users in a virtual setting further synchronized with the appropriate machine learning algorithms to interpret and explore the flood risk exposure in the first place.
Integrating User Input in Automated Object Placement for Augmented Reality
Jalal Safari Bazargani, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Soo-Mi Choi
IEEE TVCG, 2025

The pipeline employs entity recognition, object detection, depth estimation along with spawn area allocation to create a placement system.

Eliminating the Distance-Precision Trade-off in Distant Object Placement and Manipulation in Virtual Reality
Jalal Safari Bazargani, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Soo-Mi Choi
ISMAR, 2025

This paper proposes a portal method that enables users to grab nearby objects and place them at a distance, in contrast to existing approaches that focus solely on selecting and manipulating distant objects.
Beyond the Headset: Enabling Group Collaboration in Cross-Reality Environments
Jalal Safari Bazargani, Phil Joong Kim, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Soo-Mi Choi
ISMAR, 2025

We propose a hybrid AR system integrating HMDs with a large, curved display to facilitate collaboration between an HMD user and a group of non-HMD participants—including supervisors, trainees, or audience members.
Adaptive Spelling in Immersive Reality: The Impact of Gamified VR and LLMs on Young Learners’ English Color Word Acquisition
Jalal Safari Bazargani, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Xinyu Shi, Soo-Mi Choi

This study explores a VR-based, gamified approach using LLM-driven adaptive learning to improve spelling acquisition of English color words among young learners.

Alzheimer's Disease Diagnosis in the Metaverse
Jalal Safari Bazargani, Nasir Rahim, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Tamer Abuhmed, Houbing Song, Soo-Mi Choi
Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine, 2024

This paper tried to design a VR environment suitable for older adults in which three cognitive assessments namely: ADAS-Cog, Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA), and Mini Mental State Exam (MMSE), are implemented. Moreover, a 3DCNN-ML model was trained based on the corresponding cognitive tests and Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) with different modalities using the Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative 2 (ADNI2) dataset and incorporated into the application to predict if the patient suffers from AD.

Artificial intelligence applications in K-12 education: A systematic literature review
Mostafa Zafari, Jalal Safari Bazargani, Abolghasem Sadeghi-Niaraki, Soo-Mi Choi
IEEE ACCESS, 2022

In this research, the current state of the art of the integration of AI in K-12 education was provided. Specifically, different parts of education in which AI was employed along with the related AI categories were discussed according to different K-12 grades and courses. Additionally, technologies and environments that contributed to employing AI in education were discussed.
